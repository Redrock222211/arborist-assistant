Victorian planning overlays control what can be done with land and vegetation. As an arborist, understanding these overlays is **CRITICAL** for determining:

## üå≥ VEGETATION & TREE PROTECTION OVERLAYS

### **VPO - Vegetation Protection Overlay**
**What it means:**
- Protects significant vegetation on private and public land
- Requires planning permit to remove, destroy, or lop native vegetation
- May require arborist report and offset requirements

**Arborist Implications:**
- ‚úÖ Planning permit required for most tree removal
- ‚úÖ Arborist report mandatory
- ‚úÖ Offset/replanting may be required
- ‚úÖ Native vegetation assessment needed
- ‚úÖ Biodiversity assessment may be required

**Common Triggers:**
- Removing any native tree
- Removing trees >X diameter (varies by council)
- Clearing vegetation for development

**Report Requirements:**
- Tree assessment report
- Native vegetation assessment
- Offset calculations
- Biodiversity impact statement

---

### **SLO - Significant Landscape Overlay**
**What it means:**
- Protects areas with significant landscape, environmental, or cultural values
- Controls buildings, works, and vegetation removal
- Often applies to scenic areas, ridgelines, coastal areas

**Arborist Implications:**
- ‚úÖ Planning permit may be required for tree removal
- ‚úÖ Landscape impact assessment needed
- ‚úÖ Visual amenity considerations
- ‚úÖ Replacement planting often required

**Common Locations:**
- Dandenong Ranges
- Mornington Peninsula
- Coastal areas
- Scenic ridgelines
- Areas of landscape significance

**Report Requirements:**
- Landscape impact assessment
- Visual amenity report
- Replacement planting plan
- Arborist assessment

---

### **ESO - Environmental Significance Overlay**
**What it means:**
- Protects areas with environmental significance
- Includes habitat areas, waterways, wetlands
- Strict controls on vegetation removal

**Arborist Implications:**
- ‚úÖ Planning permit required for tree removal
- ‚úÖ Environmental impact assessment
- ‚úÖ Habitat assessment if applicable
- ‚úÖ Waterway protection requirements
- ‚úÖ May require ecologist involvement

**Common Features:**
- Riparian zones (waterways)
- Wetlands
- Habitat corridors
- Endangered species habitat
- Significant ecosystems

**Report Requirements:**
- Environmental impact assessment
- Habitat assessment
- Flora and fauna survey
- Waterway protection plan
- Arborist + ecologist reports

## üèõÔ∏è HERITAGE & CULTURAL OVERLAYS

### **HO - Heritage Overlay**
**What it means:**
- Protects places of heritage significance
- Controls alterations, demolition, and tree removal
- Applies to buildings, gardens, landscapes, and trees

**Arborist Implications:**
- ‚úÖ Planning permit required for tree removal
- ‚úÖ Heritage arborist report mandatory
- ‚úÖ Tree may be individually significant
- ‚úÖ Garden/landscape context important
- ‚úÖ Heritage Victoria may need to approve

**Common Applications:**
- Historic gardens
- Significant trees (e.g., 100+ years old)
- Trees associated with heritage buildings
- Memorial trees
- Trees of cultural significance

**Report Requirements:**
- Heritage arborist report
- Historical research
- Significance assessment
- Conservation management plan
- Replacement strategy (if removal approved)

**Special Considerations:**
- Some trees are individually heritage listed
- Garden context matters
- Historical plantings protected
- Replacement must be "like for like"

---

### **NCO - Neighbourhood Character Overlay**

**Arborist Implications:**
- ‚úÖ May require permit for significant tree removal
- ‚úÖ Streetscape impact assessment
- ‚úÖ Canopy cover considerations
- ‚úÖ Replacement planting expected

**Common Areas:**
- Established garden suburbs
- Tree-lined streets
- Areas with significant canopy cover

**Report Requirements:**
- Neighbourhood character assessment
- Streetscape impact report
- Canopy cover analysis
- Replacement planting plan

## üî• BUSHFIRE & RISK OVERLAYS

### **BMO - Bushfire Management Overlay**
**What it means:**
- Identifies bushfire risk areas
- Controls vegetation near buildings
- Balances fire safety with vegetation protection

**Arborist Implications:**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è May REQUIRE tree removal (defendable space)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Vegetation clearance zones around buildings
- ‚ö†Ô∏è BUT still need permit in some cases
- ‚úÖ Bushfire attack level (BAL) assessment
- ‚úÖ Defendable space requirements

**Clearance Requirements:**
- **0-10m from building:** Low-threat vegetation only
- **10-30m from building:** Managed vegetation
- **30-100m from building:** Reduced fuel loads

**Report Requirements:**
- Bushfire risk assessment
- Defendable space plan
- Vegetation management plan
- BAL assessment

**Special Note:**
- This overlay can OVERRIDE vegetation protection
- Fire safety takes precedence
- But still need proper assessment and permits

## üìú LOCAL LAWS & TREE PROTECTION

### **Local Law Tree Protection**
**What it means:**
- Council-specific tree protection
- Applies to trees over certain size (e.g., 5m height, 50cm circumference)
- Separate from planning overlays

**Common Thresholds (varies by council):**
- **Height:** 5m, 8m, or 10m
- **Circumference:** 50cm, 100cm, or 150cm at 1m height
- **Canopy spread:** 3m, 5m, or 8m

**Arborist Implications:**
- ‚úÖ Permit required even without planning overlay
- ‚úÖ Arborist report usually required
- ‚úÖ Replacement planting conditions
- ‚úÖ Check specific council local law

**Major Victorian Councils with Strong Protection:**
- City of Melbourne (very strict)
- Boroondara (comprehensive)
- Stonnington (strict)
- Bayside (coastal protection)
- Yarra Ranges (environmental focus)

## üí∞ PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE

### **Removing Trees Without Permit:**
- **Fines:** $5,000 - $500,000+ (depending on overlay and tree)
- **Prosecution:** Criminal charges possible
- **Enforcement:** Council can require replanting
- **Compensation:** May need to pay tree valuation

### **Highest Penalties:**
- Heritage trees: Up to $500,000
- Significant native vegetation: Up to $300,000
- Environmental overlay: Up to $200,000
- Standard VPO: Up to $100,000

**ALWAYS GET PERMITS!**

## üõ†Ô∏è PRACTICAL WORKFLOW FOR ARBORISTS

### **Step 1: Site Assessment**
1. Get property address
2. Check planning overlays (VicPlan or your app)
3. Check council local law
4. Check significant tree register

### **Step 2: Tree Assessment**
1. Identify species
2. Measure DBH, height, canopy
3. Assess health and structure
4. Determine retention value
5. Risk assessment (if applicable)

### **Step 3: Determine Requirements**
1. Is permit required? (overlay + local law)
2. What type of report? (standard, heritage, environmental)
3. Who needs to be involved? (arborist, heritage advisor, ecologist)
4. What standards apply? (AS4970, AS4373)

### **Step 4: Report & Application**
1. Prepare arborist report
2. Include all required assessments
3. Submit to council
4. Respond to requests for information
5. Await decision (28-60 days typical)

### **Step 5: Works (if approved)**
1. Comply with permit conditions
2. Follow AS4373 (pruning) or AS4970 (protection)
3. Implement tree protection measures
4. Document compliance
5. Arrange inspections if required

## üìö KEY AUSTRALIAN STANDARDS

### **AS4970-2009** - Protection of Trees on Development Sites
- Mandatory for development sites
- Tree Protection Zones (TPZ)
- Structural Root Zones (SRZ)
- Tree protection fencing
- Ground protection
- Arborist supervision

### **AS4373-2007** - Pruning of Amenity Trees
- Pruning standards and techniques
- Formative, remedial, and maintenance pruning
- Crown reduction, thinning, lifting
- Deadwooding and cleaning

### **AS2303-2018** - Tree Stock for Landscape Use
- Replacement tree specifications
- Quality standards
- Size and form requirements
